Chaos is pretty

Deterministic chaos means a system is unpredictable even though it is not random. We can know exactly how to calculate what happens to it yet be unable to make conclusions about its long term evolution. This can happen if tiny changes to a system now result in major differences in behaviour later on. [footnote that this is not the only condition necessary for chaos] Any measurement of the system you make now will have finite accuracy and precision and any computer will have finite precision when you use it to simulate the system. This means you might not have enough information to understand your system forever, or maybe not even for a week (which happens with weather). Deterministic chaos is many things but let's focus on a really important one; pretty.
